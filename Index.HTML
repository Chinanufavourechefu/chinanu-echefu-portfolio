Medication and IV Infusion Tracker — Designed for Patient Safety
Paper based · Adopted by the full nursing team · Became standard practice
The Problem
No standardised tracking between nursing shifts — medications and IV infusions could be missed or duplicated. In a ward setting, that is a patient safety risk.
The Result
A structured form covering oral meds, IV infusions, IM injections, and nebulisation — with a handover confirmation column. Adopted across the ward. Errors reduced.

Digital reconstruction — the ward did not use EHR systems. Original was a paper form designed and introduced by Chinanu Favour Echefu.

De-escalation Under Pressure
Real situation · Pediatric ward · Colleague collapsed from tension
What Happened
A caregiver in the pediatric ward became highly distressed. The tension escalated. A colleague became overwhelmed and collapsed. When I returned to duty, my colleagues said the situation would not have happened if I had been there.
What I Said to My Colleagues
"I am human too. Please do not count on me — I might need help someday. Learn to be calm in chaos and look for how to solve the problem, not how to prove you are right."
The Outcome
The caregiver later apologised and covered the colleague's medical costs. His son continued receiving care. The relationship was preserved — not because anyone won an argument, but because someone stayed steady.
Why This Matters for Operations Work
Calm under pressure is a skill, not a personality trait. I bring this into every client interaction, every escalation, and every difficult conversation in a business context.
